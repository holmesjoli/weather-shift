Weather shift

Bomb cyclones, Arctic blasts, Snowmaggedon. In recent years, weather terminology in the media has taken on new dimensions of drama to reflect the increasing trend in weather extremes.

Co-opting technical meteorological terms to describe the weather for a general audience is unhelpful at best and outright misleading at worst. Nevertheless, media outlets continue to borrow these highly technical terms hoping to lend credibility to their reporting by utilizing jargon. Furthermore, technical terms add an element of drama that furthers storytelling. For example, the term "polar vortex" is used in everyday parlance to describe abnormally cold weather. In scientific literature, the "polar vortex" refers to the eastward flow of high altitude air around the poles. While technical wording can lend weather reports a veneer of scientific credibility, media outlets often use weather terms in reductionistic ways, heightening the risk of inaccuracies.
